Abstract


Generating high-quality novel views of a scene from a single image requires maintaining spatial consistency across different viewpoints, commonly referred to as view consistency. While diffusion models have advanced the task of novel view synthesis, they struggle to preserve spatial coherence across views. To address this, diffusion models have been combined with 3D models, but such approaches often lack efficiency for practical use due to their complex multi-step pipelines. In this work, we propose a training-free method which utilizes diffusion models to overcome this limitation without relying on additional models. Our key idea is to enhance diffusion models by leveraging view-guided warping, enabling adaptive attention manipulation and noise re-initialization to mitigate view inconsistency. Through comprehensive metrics and extensive quantitative evaluations and generation results, we demonstrate that our method achieves view consistency, which maintains structural coherence across viewpoints within a scene. Furthermore, our approach is adaptable to various diffusion models, demonstrating its broader applicability.

Method


Given an input view , depth map and continuous camera poses, our method generates scene images with smooth view transitions. (b) Pose-aware noise initialization re-initializes the diffusion initial noise by leveraging warped images and initial noise, incorporating frequency domain information. (c) Warp-Guided Adaptive Attention utilizes the warped region mask and reference range obtained from the Adaptive Warp-Range selection, performing masked batch self-attention on the warped region mask and reference range. Thus, (a) our method warps the input view according to the continuous camera poses and the depth map, processes it through both Pose-Aware Noise Initialization and Warp-Guided Adaptive Attention, and generates consistent novel view images.



Baseline Comparison

In this paper, we evaluate our method against the selected baselines, MegaScenes and VistaDream, by generating continuous viewpoints from arbitrary input images. Since VistaDream utilizes rendering techniques, it naturally produces smooth transitions between viewpoints but introduces artifacts in the background. On the other hand, MegaScenes struggles to maintain consistency across different viewpoints. In contrast, our method, WAVE, achieves high consistency and seamless transitions without relying on rendering techniques.





Different Pose NVS with WAVE




By applying our proposed method, we generate results across multiple camera poses, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling different viewpoints. Additionally, to further illustrate its capability, we perform additional generations along another pose trajectory, providing diverse examples of novel view synthesis.

Consistency & Camera Accuracy Metrics




We conduct experiments on MegaScenes, DTU, and Re10K and evaluate consis- tency using two metrics: First metric, which measures consistency with the first frame, and Next metric, which assesses consistency with neighboring viewpoints. Additionally, to evaluate camera accuracy, a crucial aspect of novel view synthesis, we report the accuracy of camera extrinsic parameters using Frobenius Norm, Rotation Angle, and Angular Consistency.
